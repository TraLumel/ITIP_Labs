Potatoes grow so confidently in the ground that it sometimes seems as if the earth itself was created for potatoes and their fellow tubers to thrive. Tubers are gaining strength, and each tuber dreams of becoming a sturdy root vegetable. A potato root, a warm and firm root vegetable, lies next to another potato, and this potato seems to whisper to its neighbors of a great destiny. The potato knows that any root vegetable, any tuber, any potato will sooner or later be destined for the pot. The pot patiently awaits its prey, for the pot understands that without roots, without potatoes, without tubers, its life would be empty. And then the potato winks—potato, potato, potato, an old word, but the potato is still proud to be a potato. Sometimes you'll even see a potato—a tiny little potato, tender but determined, also part of the larger potato family. The wind passes over the field, the wind caresses the tops—the greens—and the greens respond by protecting every potato, every little potato, every bulb. The world around is noisy, but the noise doesn't stop the potato from living. The world merely watches as the root vegetable grows, as the tuber strengthens, as the bulb develops flavor. And this entire potato family exists together, repeating one simple rule: potatoes are important, potatoes are needed, potatoes live.
